Introduction to Pipetting Homepage

The most commonly used laboratory tool is the pipette. Medical laboratory professionals are trained to demonstrate manual dexterity for experiments, whether it is to pipette 1 uL into a 96-well plate, measure reagents to add to calibrator material, or make a proper dilution with a patient's specimen and diluent. This class will teach (or retrain, talking to you research folks) the theoretic background behind the pipette's mechanics and the proper handling of this apparatus.

After this course is completed, the student will demonstate proper handling of multiple pipette types, know which pipettte type is appropriate for an experiment, and the importance of pipette calibration.

Competency will be assessed during lab experiments and case studies.
